\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Pricing American-style options requires implied volatility (IV) as an input, yet IV itself is an empirical quantity extracted from observed option prices~\cite{black1973}. This circularity poses a practical problem for synthetic data generation: existing approaches either take IV from the market and feed it into a pricing model, or generate price paths without a consistent IV surface. The result is a scarcity of realistic synthetic option data for training machine-learning models, backtesting trading strategies, and conducting scenario-based risk analysis.

In this study, we addressed this gap by developing a framework in which IV is an \emph{output} of a structural model of equity returns, not an input. The pipeline chained three stages: (i)~a Jump Hidden Markov Model (JumpHMM)~\cite{jumphmm2025} produced multi-asset price paths with realistic heavy tails, negligible linear autocorrelation, and persistent volatility clustering, with cross-asset dependence imposed via a Student-$t$ copula; (ii)~a modified Heston stochastic variance process~\cite{heston1993} converted those price paths into IV paths, where the mean-reversion target $\theta$ was a function of the HMM regime state, days to expiration (DTE), moneyness, and an aggregate market mood indicator; and (iii)~a Cox--Ross--Rubinstein (CRR) binomial tree~\cite{cox1979} converted the pre-computed IV into American option prices with early exercise. The central innovation was a hybrid $\theta$-function that linked the Heston mean-reversion target to the JumpHMM regime state, producing regime-dependent volatility dynamics (Section~\ref{sec:theta_function}). An equilibrium initialization $v_0 = \theta(t\!=\!0)$ gave each (ticker, strike, DTE) triple its own initial IV, so the smile, skew, and term structure emerged automatically without external IV data (Section~\ref{sec:initialization}). An endogenous market mood signal, defined as the fraction of tickers in tail HMM states, modulated IV across all contracts without introducing circularity (Section~\ref{sec:mood}). We calibrated the five-parameter smile and term-structure function $\psi$ on real NVDA option chains across seven expirations, capturing the U-shaped ATM term structure with an RMSE of 1.15\% IV, and demonstrated that the smile shape generalized across AMD, MU, and INTC with only a scalar level adjustment per ticker (Section~\ref{sec:calibration}). The framework is implemented as an open-source Julia package designed to serve as a synthetic option market generator for downstream applications.
